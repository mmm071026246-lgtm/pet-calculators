Cat Water Intake Calculator

Estimate your cat's daily water needs based on body weight. Water requirements may vary depending on diet, activity level, age, environment, and health conditions.



Daily Water Requirements

Most healthy cats require approximately 50 to 60 milliliters of water per kilogram of body weight each day. This calculator uses 55 ml per kilogram as a general estimate.

Why Hydration Is Important

Proper hydration supports kidney function, digestion, temperature regulation, and overall health. Cats that primarily eat dry food may require additional water intake.

Signs of Dehydration in Cats

Common signs of dehydration include lethargy, dry gums, reduced appetite, sunken eyes, and decreased skin elasticity. If you suspect dehydration, contact your veterinarian.

Important Note

This calculator provides a general estimate only. Consult your veterinarian if you have concerns about your cat's hydration or health.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should a cat drink per day?

Most healthy cats require approximately 50 to 60 ml of water per kilogram of body weight daily.

Why is my cat drinking more water than usual?

Increased thirst can sometimes be caused by diet, weather, activity level, or underlying medical conditions.

Do cats eating wet food need less water?

Yes. Wet food contains significant moisture and may reduce the amount of drinking water required.

Can dehydration be dangerous for cats?

Yes. Severe dehydration can lead to serious health complications and requires veterinary attention.

How can I encourage my cat to drink more water?

Fresh water, multiple water bowls, water fountains, and wet food may help increase water intake.

Should I follow my veterinarian's advice?

Yes. This calculator provides estimates only and should not replace professional veterinary guidance.
