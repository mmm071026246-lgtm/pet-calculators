How Much Water Should a Cat Drink Daily?

Water plays a critical role in a cat's overall health. Proper hydration supports kidney function, digestion, circulation, and body temperature regulation. Understanding your cat's daily water needs can help prevent dehydration and related health problems.

General Water Intake Recommendations

Most healthy cats require approximately 50 to 60 milliliters of water per kilogram of body weight each day. For example, a 4 kg cat may need around 200 to 240 ml of water daily.

Factors That Affect Water Consumption

A cat's water needs can vary depending on diet, age, activity level, weather conditions, and overall health. Cats living in hot climates or highly active cats may require additional water.

Dry Food and Water Intake

Cats that primarily eat dry food often need to drink more water because kibble contains very little moisture. Cats eating wet food may obtain a significant portion of their daily hydration from their meals.

Signs of Dehydration

  • Dry gums
  • Low energy levels
  • Loss of appetite
  • Sunken eyes
  • Reduced skin elasticity

If severe dehydration is suspected, contact a veterinarian immediately.

Ways to Encourage Drinking

  • Provide fresh water daily.
  • Use multiple water bowls around the home.
  • Consider a cat water fountain.
  • Offer wet food when appropriate.
  • Keep water bowls clean.

Why Hydration Is Important

Adequate hydration helps support urinary tract health and kidney function, both of which are important concerns for many cats as they age.
