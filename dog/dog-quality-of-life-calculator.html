Understanding Quality of Life

Quality of life assessments are commonly used by pet owners and veterinarians to monitor comfort, mobility, enjoyment, and daily functioning, particularly for senior dogs or dogs with chronic illnesses.

How Scores Are Interpreted

  • 15–18: Good quality of life
  • 10–14: Moderate quality of life
  • 0–9: Reduced quality of life

This tool is for educational purposes only and should not replace professional veterinary advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a quality of life assessment?

It is a structured way to evaluate a dog's comfort, happiness, appetite, and ability to perform daily activities.

Can this calculator determine medical treatment?

No. It is intended as an educational assessment and not a medical diagnosis.

Who should use this calculator?

Owners of senior dogs, dogs with chronic illness, or dogs recovering from injury may find it useful.
