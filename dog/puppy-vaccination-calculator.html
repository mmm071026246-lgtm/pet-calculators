Typical Puppy Vaccine Timeline

Most puppies receive a series of vaccinations during the first few months of life to help protect against common infectious diseases.

  • 6–8 weeks: First vaccinations
  • 10–12 weeks: Booster vaccinations
  • 14–16 weeks: Final puppy boosters

Why Puppy Vaccinations Matter

Vaccinations help protect puppies from serious diseases while their immune systems are still developing. Keeping vaccinations up to date is an important part of responsible pet ownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should puppies receive their first vaccines?

Most puppies begin vaccinations between 6 and 8 weeks of age.

How many puppy vaccine visits are required?

Most puppies receive multiple vaccine visits until approximately 16 weeks of age.

Can vaccination schedules vary?

Yes. Your veterinarian may recommend a different schedule depending on local disease risks and your puppy's overall health.
