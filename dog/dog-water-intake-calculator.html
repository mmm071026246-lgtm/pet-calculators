Dog Water Intake Calculator

Estimate your dog's daily water needs based on body weight. Water requirements vary depending on age, activity level, weather, diet, and overall health.



Daily Water Requirements

Healthy dogs typically require between 50 and 60 milliliters of water per kilogram of body weight per day. This calculator uses 55 ml per kilogram as a general guideline.

Why Hydration Matters

Proper hydration supports digestion, circulation, temperature regulation, joint health, and overall well-being.

Signs of Dehydration in Dogs

  • Dry nose or gums
  • Excessive panting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Low energy levels
  • Sunken eyes
  • Reduced skin elasticity
Important: If your dog drinks significantly more or less water than normal, consult a veterinarian.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should a dog drink daily?

Most dogs need approximately 50–60 ml of water per kilogram of body weight each day.

Do puppies need more water?

Puppies may require more frequent access to water due to growth and activity levels.

Can dry food increase water needs?

Yes. Dogs eating dry kibble generally drink more water than dogs eating wet food.

Can dogs drink too much water?

Excessive thirst may indicate an underlying health issue and should be evaluated by a veterinarian.
