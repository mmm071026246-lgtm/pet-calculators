How the Calculator Works

Many raw feeding guidelines recommend feeding between 2% and 3.5% of a dog's body weight daily depending on age, activity level, metabolism, and body condition.

Typical Raw Feeding Ratios

  • Low Activity: 2% of body weight
  • Average Activity: 2.5% of body weight
  • Active Dogs: 3% of body weight
  • Very Active Dogs: 3.5% of body weight

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a raw diet for dogs?

A raw diet typically consists of uncooked meat, bones, organs, and selected fruits or vegetables.

How much raw food should a dog eat daily?

Most adult dogs eat between 2% and 3.5% of their body weight per day depending on activity level and metabolism.

Is raw feeding suitable for all dogs?

Dietary needs vary between dogs. Consult a veterinarian before making significant dietary changes.

Can puppies eat raw food?

Puppies have different nutritional requirements and often require higher feeding percentages than adult dogs.

What is BARF?

BARF stands for Biologically Appropriate Raw Food or Bones and Raw Food, a popular raw feeding approach.
