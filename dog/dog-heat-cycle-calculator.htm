How the Dog Heat Cycle Calculator Works

Most female dogs enter heat approximately every 6 months, although timing can vary by breed, age, and individual health.

Ovulation often occurs around days 10–14 after the heat cycle begins, making this period the most fertile time for breeding.

Typical Heat Cycle Stages

  • Proestrus: Days 1–9
  • Estrus (Fertile Period): Days 10–14
  • Diestrus: Around 60 days
  • Anestrus: Resting phase until next cycle

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do dogs go into heat?

Most dogs experience heat cycles about every six months, although some breeds vary.

When is a dog most fertile?

Many dogs are most fertile between days 10 and 14 after the start of their heat cycle.

How long does a heat cycle last?

A typical heat cycle lasts approximately 2 to 4 weeks.

Can heat cycles vary by breed?

Yes. Smaller breeds often cycle more frequently, while giant breeds may cycle less often.

Is this calculator accurate?

The calculator provides estimates based on common canine reproductive timelines. Individual dogs may vary.
