Dog Body Condition Score Calculator

Use this free Dog Body Condition Score (BCS) Calculator to estimate your dog's weight status. Veterinarians commonly use the 9-point BCS system to evaluate body fat and overall body condition.




Understanding Body Condition Scores

The Body Condition Score system evaluates a dog's body fat using visible and physical characteristics such as waist shape, abdominal tuck, and rib visibility.

  • BCS 1-3: Underweight
  • BCS 4-5: Ideal Weight
  • BCS 6-7: Overweight
  • BCS 8-9: Obese

How to Check Your Dog's Body Condition

  • You should be able to feel the ribs without pressing hard.
  • A visible waist should be present when viewed from above.
  • The abdomen should tuck upward behind the rib cage.
  • Excess fat deposits may indicate overweight status.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Dog Body Condition Score?

Body Condition Score (BCS) is a veterinary tool used to assess whether a dog is underweight, ideal weight, overweight, or obese.

What is the ideal Body Condition Score?

Most veterinarians consider scores of 4 or 5 on a 9-point scale to represent an ideal body condition.

Can this replace veterinary advice?

No. This calculator provides general guidance only and should not replace professional veterinary evaluation.
