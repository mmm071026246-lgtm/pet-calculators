Signs Your Dog Is Overweight

Maintaining a healthy weight is one of the most important factors in a dog's overall health. Excess body weight can increase the risk of diabetes, joint problems, heart disease, and reduced quality of life.

Difficulty Feeling the Ribs

One of the easiest ways to assess your dog's body condition is by feeling the ribs. You should be able to feel the ribs without pressing hard. If the ribs are difficult to feel, your dog may be overweight.

Loss of a Visible Waist

When viewed from above, a healthy dog usually has a noticeable waist behind the ribs. If the body appears round or oval-shaped, excess weight may be present.

Reduced Activity Levels

Overweight dogs often become tired more quickly and may show less interest in exercise or play. They may also have difficulty keeping up during walks.

Breathing Difficulties

Excess weight can place additional stress on the respiratory system. Some overweight dogs pant more frequently, even during mild activity.

Joint and Mobility Problems

Extra body weight increases stress on joints and may worsen conditions such as arthritis. Dogs may have difficulty climbing stairs, jumping, or standing up.

Common Causes of Weight Gain

  • Overfeeding
  • Too many treats
  • Lack of exercise
  • Age-related metabolism changes
  • Certain medical conditions

How to Help Your Dog Lose Weight

  • Measure food portions carefully.
  • Increase daily exercise gradually.
  • Limit high-calorie treats.
  • Monitor weight regularly.
  • Consult a veterinarian when needed.

Why a Healthy Weight Matters

Dogs that maintain a healthy body weight often live longer and experience fewer health problems throughout their lives.
