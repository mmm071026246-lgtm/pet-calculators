How Much Food Should I Feed My Cat?

Providing the right amount of food is one of the most important parts of caring for a cat. Feeding too much can lead to obesity, while feeding too little may result in poor nutrition and weight loss.

Factors That Affect Food Requirements

Every cat is different. Daily food requirements depend on body weight, age, activity level, metabolism, and overall health. Indoor cats often need fewer calories than highly active outdoor cats.

Kittens Need More Energy

Kittens grow rapidly and require more calories relative to their body weight than adult cats. They are typically fed multiple small meals throughout the day.

Adult Cats

Adult cats generally require a balanced diet that maintains a healthy body weight. Portion sizes should be adjusted if weight gain or weight loss occurs.

Senior Cats

Older cats may have different nutritional needs. Some senior cats require fewer calories due to reduced activity, while others may need specialized diets recommended by a veterinarian.

Signs of Overfeeding

  • Steady weight gain
  • Difficulty feeling the ribs
  • Reduced activity levels
  • Excess body fat around the abdomen

Signs of Underfeeding

  • Weight loss
  • Visible ribs or spine
  • Low energy levels
  • Poor coat condition

Monitor Your Cat's Body Condition

A healthy cat should have a visible waist when viewed from above and ribs that can be felt without excessive pressure. Regular weight checks can help identify problems early.
