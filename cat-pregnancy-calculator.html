How Long Are Cats Pregnant?

The average feline pregnancy lasts approximately 63 to 67 days, with 65 days commonly used as an estimate.

Cat Pregnancy Timeline

Pregnant cats typically begin showing physical and behavioral changes within a few weeks after mating. Proper nutrition, veterinary care, and monitoring are important throughout pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a cat pregnant?

Most cats are pregnant for approximately 63 to 67 days, with 65 days commonly used as an average estimate.

How accurate is a cat pregnancy calculator?

A cat pregnancy calculator provides an estimated due date based on the mating date. Actual delivery dates may vary.

How can I tell if my cat is pregnant?

Common signs include increased appetite, weight gain, enlarged nipples, and behavioral changes.

When should I take my pregnant cat to the veterinarian?

You should contact a veterinarian if you suspect pregnancy or notice unusual symptoms during gestation.

How many kittens does a cat usually have?

The average litter size is typically between three and five kittens, although larger or smaller litters are possible.

Can first-time cats have healthy pregnancies?

Yes. Many first-time mothers have normal pregnancies, but veterinary care is recommended for monitoring health and development.
