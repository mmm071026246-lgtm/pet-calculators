How Much Food Should I Feed My Dog?

Feeding the correct amount of food is important for maintaining a healthy weight and supporting your dog's overall well-being. Overfeeding may lead to obesity, while underfeeding can result in nutritional deficiencies.

Food Requirements Depend on Several Factors

The amount of food a dog needs depends on body weight, age, activity level, breed, metabolism, and health status. Active dogs typically require more calories than less active dogs.

Puppies vs Adult Dogs

Puppies require more calories relative to their body weight because they are growing rapidly. Adult dogs generally require fewer calories and should be fed according to their maintenance needs.

Body Weight Guidelines

Many feeding recommendations are based on body weight. As a general estimate, adult dogs may consume approximately 2% to 3% of their body weight in food per day, depending on the type of diet.

Signs You May Be Feeding Too Much

  • Unexplained weight gain
  • Difficulty feeling the ribs
  • Reduced activity levels
  • Increased body fat around the abdomen

Signs You May Be Feeding Too Little

  • Weight loss
  • Visible ribs or spine
  • Low energy levels
  • Poor coat condition

Monitor Body Condition

Instead of focusing only on weight, monitor your dog's overall body condition. A healthy dog should have a visible waist and ribs that can be felt without excessive pressure.
