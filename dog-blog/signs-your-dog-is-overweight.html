Signs Your Dog Is Overweight

Maintaining a healthy weight is essential for your dog's overall health. Excess weight increases the risk of diabetes, joint issues, and heart disease.

Difficulty Feeling the Ribs

You should be able to feel your dog's ribs easily. If not, your dog may be overweight.

Loss of a Visible Waist

A healthy dog has a visible waist when viewed from above. A round body shape may indicate excess fat.

Reduced Activity Levels

Overweight dogs tire easily and may avoid exercise.

Breathing Difficulties

Extra weight can strain breathing and increase panting.

Joint Problems

Extra weight puts pressure on joints and can worsen mobility.

Common Causes

  • Overfeeding
  • Too many treats
  • Lack of exercise
  • Age-related changes

How to Help Your Dog Lose Weight

  • Measure food carefully
  • Increase activity
  • Limit treats
  • Track progress
Tip: Even small weight loss can significantly improve your dog's health.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if my dog is overweight?

Difficulty feeling ribs and lack of waist are key signs.

Yes, obesity increases health risks.

How can I help my dog lose weight?

Control diet, increase exercise, and monitor progress.
